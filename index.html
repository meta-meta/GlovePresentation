how to make an incredibly cheap pressure/bend sensor

Materials
Velostat - Resistence decreases with pressure
http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00BEKMVX4/ref=cm_cr_ryp_prd_ttl_sol_0
$15 for 100 bags
Start by placing the lead on one side
tape it down with copper tape. conductive adhesive
same on the opposite side
cut to size
wrap in duct tape
finished?

WRONG

Conductive adhesive is not that conductive.
Pressure sensing works great where the wires are.
Not so well on the rest of the sensor.

Redesign

This approach is almost the same, but notice the two extra pieces of copper tape.
this time we'll start by taping the copper to the center of gorilla tape
so that the non-adhesive side is in contact with the velostat
place lead at end of copper tape
secure it with a little tab of copper tape.
alternately you can solder to copper tape
place strip of velostat over copper tape so that it's completely covered
this highlights another mistake. don't let that copper tape stick out
repeat the first two steps for the other side
join the two together being careful that the velostat completely covers the copper tape
test with multimeter. 134 ohms resting position
drops down to 13.4 ohms with force applied

How do we make this mean something to the Arduino?

Voltage Divider

R2: Bend Sensor

R1: Appropriately Matched Resistor for Sensor Range

Vout = Vin × R2 / (R1 + R2)

Vin: +5V

Vout: ~1-4V
< optimal range but good enough for me

Arduino ADC takes 0 - 5v
this consumes some power. could be more efficient.
arduino offers 10bit (1024) adc. this circuit utilizes about 60% of that
we could use another voltage divider and an amplifier to increase the range but be very careful not to go above around 5.5V as it will destroy your I/O pin
